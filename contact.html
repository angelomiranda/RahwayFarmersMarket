    Brief History

    Rahway Farmers Market was founded in 1976 with a two-fold mission: to promote regional agriculture by providing small family farms the opportunity to sell their locally grown products directly to consumers, and to ensure that all New Jersey Residents have access to the freshest, most nutritious locally grown food the region has to offer.

    What began over three decades ago with 12 farmers in a parking lot on Main Street and 3rd Avenue in Rayway has now grown to become the largest and most diverse outdoor urban farmers market network in the country, now with 54 markets, over 230 family farms and fishermen participating, and over 30,000 acres of farmland protected from development.

    In 2010, GrowNJ and the Mayor's Fund to Advance New Jersey launched Grow to Learn: The Citywide School Gardens Initiative, a one stop resource for any public school looking to build, sustain, and program a school garden. See what kind of gardens OSG has been building to support the Grow to Learn mission.

    Our Grow Truck program delivers free tool loans to neighborhood greening projects and our Annual Plant Sale provides at-cost Greenmarket vegetable, herb, and flower starters to community gardens, schools, and block associations.

    GrowNJ brings the freshest, highest quality products to New Jersey's wholesale buyers: packing, organizing and delivering pallets of regionally grown fresh fruits and vegetables to grocery stores, bodegas, restaurants, and other retail outlets throughout New Jersey City. By providing delivery and brokering services, We are reducing barriers for buyers and farmers which produce from the farm to the store.

    Rahway Farmers Market builds on what GrowNJ has been doing for the last 35 years via the celebrated Greenmarket farmers markets. Farmers markets are thriving, but they do not match every farm business model and farmers market purchases are a small percentage of food consumed in New Jersey.

    Distribution of locally grown farm products via wholesale channels such as grocery stores and institutional kitchens greatly increases New Jersey access to locally grown products and helps keep regional farm businesses viable. Our Market promotes regional agriculture by providing small family farms the opportunity to sell their locally grown products directly to consumers.

    The Rahway Farmers Market offers over 100 local and regional farm-fresh products including fruits, vegetables, herbs, plants, and flowers at competitive wholesale prices and quantities.

    In addition to working with high-end restaurants and caterers, Rahway Market delivers products to corner grocers and GrowNJ food access programs such as Youthmarket, ensuring that nutritious, local products are available in neighborhoods with few fresh food outlets.
